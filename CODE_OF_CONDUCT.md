# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

ArcWren contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to make participation respectful, safe, and productive. Constructive disagreement about code, design, evidence, and tradeoffs is welcome. Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption, and publication of another person's private information are not acceptable.

Examples of positive participation include:

- criticizing ideas and changes without attacking people;
- stating assumptions and evidence clearly;
- accepting correction and taking responsibility for mistakes;
- respecting boundaries and the privacy of users and contributors.

## Scope

This standard applies in project spaces and when someone publicly represents the project. It also applies to private project communication used for contribution, moderation, or security response.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers through the repository's private security-reporting channel described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Conduct reports are not vulnerability reports, but that channel provides a private initial contact when no separate moderation address is published. Do not include unrelated credentials or private traces.

Maintainers will review reports in context, protect reporter privacy as far as practical, and may remove content, issue a warning, restrict participation, or permanently exclude a participant. Bad-faith or knowingly false reports may receive the same response. Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented privately, and free of conflicts of interest.
